Gambling

Gambling has been around for a pretty long time, since 2300 BC where some evidence of keno slips were found in China. You may think that gambling is not good for your wallet, but it has funded many state works in the past such as the construction of the Great Wall of China. The establishment of Harvard and Yale were also made possible with lottery funds. Even the Ancient Greeks and Romans loved gambling! Fun fact: The earliest casino was in Italy during the 17th century!



The History of Gambling


3000 BC Dice

There is evidence that the Mesopotamians made dice out of sheep bones, mainly the knucklebones. Throughout historical manuscripts, gambling dice have been mentioned; including the Indian Vedas and the Bible.

2300 BC Keno slips

Fill out a card → Give it to a Keno worker → you’ll receive a new ticket with numbers → Watch the Keno drawing to see if your numbers match. (The more numbers you have that match, the more money you win)

500 BC Tesserae

Romans used to roll a six-sided dice called Tesserae. Highest number would win.

800 AD Cards

Although the exact rules for the playing cards have been lost throughout history, the first cards are found to be in China around the 9th century.

1400 AD Baccarat/Baccara

The oldest game that’s still played in casinos today is Baccarat! It’s played by comparing cards between the “player” and the “banker” where people either bet on the “player” or the “banker.” The player and the banker must get as close to 9 without getting higher with their card.

1638 AD First Casinos

Italy is home to the earliest casinos during the 17th century. As an example, the Ridotto provided an environment for controlled gambling in 1638. Casinos then appeared more in Europe during the 19th century. In the US, rich farmers and traders that went through the Mississippi River by steamboats had a venue for many informal gambling.

1880 Casino Chips

Referred to as Casino Tokens by some, casino chips are basically currency for casinos. They can be used online and in-person.

1941 Hotel casinos

The El Rancho Vegas opened in a section of Highway 91. Soon, many other hotel-casinos followed. Now, that section of the highway is called The Las Vegas Strip.

1994 Online

Gambling has finally gone online! Players can now gamble on the internet making it worth billions of dollars. In 2003, Playtech released the first live dealer casino. Today, thousands of online casinos make online gaming a multibillion dollar industry. New Jersey also legalized online gambling in 2011 with more people interested in gambling. State by State, online gambling has started to be more legalized in America; America can also see an increase in mobile gambling.
